Lecture 35: GMRES¶

GMRES is simply the replacement of the problem "solve $b-Ax=0$ with "minimize $\|b-Ax\|$ over $\mathcal{K}_n$". Its convergence depends strongly on (at least) the spectrum of $A$.

This example is adapted from the text.

In [1]:
m = 1200;
A = 2*eye(m) + 0.5*randn(m,m)/sqrt(m);
plot(eig(A),'.')
axis equal, title('Eigenvalues of A')

We're going to do a lazy GMRES here. First we run Arnoldi, then successively solve the approximate least squares problems using brute force.

In [6]:
b = ones(m,1);
[Q,H] = arnoldi(A,b,15);
resnorm = [];
for n = 1:15
    e1 = eye(n+1,1);
    yn = H(1:n+1,1:n) \ (norm(b)*e1);
    resnorm(n) = norm( H(1:n+1,1:n)*yn - norm(b)*e1 );
    xn = Q(:,1:n)*yn;
end

semilogy(resnorm,'o-')
xlabel('n'), ylabel('norm of residual')
title('Convergence of GMRES residual')

The text explains why this might be very close to $4^{-n}\|b\|$.

In [8]:
semilogy(resnorm,'o-')
hold on
semilogy([1 15],norm(b)*.25.^([1 15]),'--')
xlabel('n'), ylabel('norm of residual')
title('Convergence of GMRES residual')

The rate is tied to how easy it is to make a polynomial small on the spectrum of $A$ while satisfying the normalization $p(0)=1$. If the set gets close to or surrounds the origin, this becomes difficult.

In [9]:
theta = linspace(0,pi,m)';
d = (-2+2*sin(theta)) + 1i*cos(theta);
D = diag(d);
A = A + D;
lam = eig(A);
plot(lam,'.')
axis equal, title('Eigenvalues of A')

There is again a linear convergence rate (mainly because the matrix is close to normal and its spectrum uniformly covers the set), but a much slower one this time due to the shape of the spectrum.
